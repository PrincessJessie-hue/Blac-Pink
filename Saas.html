Case Study · 2023

Medi-go SaaS.

Easy Healthcare for All. A SaaS dashboard that turns dense medical records into scannable, actionable interfaces for clinicians ,built to strict accessibility standards.

The Problem

Easy Healthcare for All.

Clinicians spend an average of 34% of their shift navigating software rather than caring for patients. The legacy system required 11 clicks to access a patient's current medication list. Medi-go redesigns the core clinical workflow from the ground up ,prioritising speed, legibility, and WCAG AA accessibility.

SaaS overview image

Context

Long, complicated admin processes take time away from the people that need it most.

A digitally-thriving platform is a proven process for working to ensure and grow a steady increase in your organisation's performance, based on accessible infrastructure for patients from all walks of life.

A digitally-inclusive system creates an environment that enables patients to conveniently access healthcare services according to their individual needs, removing the barriers of geography, language, and digital literacy.

Research

Clinicians, Not Users

40 hours of contextual inquiry embedded with clinical staff at two public hospitals, observing patient handovers, ward rounds, and triage. 16 structured interviews with doctors, nurses, and admin staff.

Findings

Key barriers identified

  • 01
    11 clicks to access medication list
    The legacy system buried critical data behind multiple layers of navigation, costing clinicians minutes per patient.
  • 02
    8-minute auto-logout
    Frequent session expiry was the single most-cited frustration across all 16 interviews.
  • 03
    Touch targets too small
    Data entry errors were 3× higher on 7" tablets due to touch targets as small as 18px.

Design Response

How we solved it

  • 01
    3-click patient profile
    Any critical metric — meds, allergies, vitals, bloods — accessible within 3 interactions from anywhere.
  • 02
    Persistent session
    Replaced 8-minute timeouts with biometric re-auth, eliminating the primary workflow interruption.
  • 03
    44px minimum touch targets
    All interactive elements rebuilt to WCAG 2.5.5 standard ,reducing tablet entry errors by 68%.
